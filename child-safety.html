Hooly - Child Safety Standards (CSAE)

Last updated: 2026-01-19

Our commitment

Hooly is a keyboard and messaging assistant. We do not operate a social network, dating platform, or public content feed. We are committed to protecting children and strictly prohibit any content that involves child sexual abuse or exploitation (CSAE).

Prohibited content

We do not allow the use of our app or services to create, request, share, or facilitate CSAE in any form. This includes any sexual content involving minors, grooming, or attempts to solicit such content.

Reporting and response

If we receive a report of CSAE-related activity involving our app or services, we will take appropriate action, which may include restricting access to AI features and cooperating with law enforcement as required by applicable law.

User reporting

If you suspect abuse or exploitation, please report it to the appropriate authorities in your country. In the United States, reports can be made to the National Center for Missing & Exploited Children (NCMEC) at https://report.cybertip.org/ .

Data handling

Hooly processes user-provided text and optional screenshots only to generate message suggestions. We do not provide public posting or sharing features within the app.
